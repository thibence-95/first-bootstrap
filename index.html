My favourite Blues artists

Robert Johnson
1911 - 1938

Robert Johnson is perhaps the most notorious Blues player thanks to the rumors of him obtaining legendary talent in no time after allegedly selling his soul to the devil at the crossroads. It's a given that his music has a sinister taste to it.
Can it get any Bluesy-er?

Listen
B.B. King
1925 - 2015

This man is the embodiment of many legendary Blues guitarists and singers in one. Just listen to a live rendition of Thrill is Gone and you'll hear, see and even feel it pour out of his soul.

Listen
Howlin' Wolf
1910 - 1976

Howlin' Wolf's style is pretty self explanatory. Zoning out on his cadances is something many have found easy to do for hours.
To me, his music is synonimous to my first experience with the Blues having perfectly seasoned omelette sandwiches with my dad at his place on rainy weekend mid-mornings, checking out his records, casettes etc. Just me and my dad at the dinner table, with our mouths stuffed, body-nodding like a couple of idiots losing our shit to licks that straight up bite.

Freddie King
1934 - 1976

Freddie, Freddie, Freddie.. Probably as soulful as his grandmomma's cookin'.

Otis Spann
1930 - 1970

If you say "Country Boy" three times in the mirror while spinning in an unlit room with a piano present Otis Spann will appear right behand you to teach you some hard-ass piano licks. No, seriously, this piano-god is my daily inspiration. I'll be damned if I can even get close to his playing style.

Listen
Blind Lemon Jefferson
1893 - 1929

If you've ever wondered where today's music actually comes from, this is what you're looking for. Blind Lemon Jefferson lived from 1893 to 1929 so it's safe to say that his music, although simple as a glass of water, had some of the biggest influence on modern music.

Listen
Muddy Waters
1913 - 1983

Blues is not only what the name says it is. The Blues can also be uplifting and heartening. Muddy waters will lift you up no matter where you are or how you feel. Try Mannish Boy and you'll hear what I hear when I think of The Blues.
